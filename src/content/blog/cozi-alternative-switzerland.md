## Why Swiss families look for a Cozi alternative

Cozi is one of the most recognized family organizer apps in the world, and for good reason: its shared calendar and shopping list are simple and genuinely useful. But for families in Switzerland, several friction points come up consistently.

**No meaningful multilingual support.** Switzerland has four official languages — German, French, Italian, and Romansh — and many Swiss families are multilingual within a single household. Cozi's interface is English-only, with limited localization. An au pair who speaks French, children in German-speaking school, and an Italian-speaking grandparent in the same family group is a real scenario in Switzerland. Cozi was not built for this.

**Advertising on the free plan.** Cozi's free tier is ad-supported. For a tool that holds your children's school schedules, pickup times, and family routines, displaying ads is a significant privacy trade-off. Under Swiss data protection law (revDSG) and the EU's GDPR framework, families are increasingly aware of how their data is used. An ad-supported family app means your household data is processed to serve targeted ads — even if Cozi's privacy policy is relatively straightforward by US standards.

**No Swiss school calendar integration.** Switzerland has 26 cantons, each with its own school holiday calendar. Cozi does not include Swiss public holidays or cantonal school holiday data. Families in Zürich, Bern, and Lausanne each follow different school calendars — and manually entering all of those into a US family app is tedious. Our guide on [school holidays Switzerland and which app handles them best](/resources/blog/school-holidays-switzerland-app) covers this in detail.

**Task assignment is underdeveloped.** Cozi's task lists are family-level, not person-level. You can add a task to the family list, but you cannot easily assign it to a specific person and send that person a reminder. For a household where two working parents need to know exactly who is doing what, this creates friction.

## What to look for in a Cozi alternative for Switzerland

When evaluating a Cozi replacement, Swiss families should look for a combination of features that most single-purpose apps do not offer:

**A genuinely multilingual interface.** Not just translated labels, but full support for German, French, Italian, and English — so every family member can use the app in their preferred language within the same family group.

**Ad-free by design.** A privacy-first approach means no advertising, no data selling, and no behavioural profiling. This is non-negotiable for families who take the Swiss revDSG seriously.

**Individual task assignment with targeted reminders.** The family to-do list should not go to everyone — it should reach the right person at the right time. When a task belongs to one parent, that parent receives the reminder, not the whole household.

**Integrated family communication.** Switching between a calendar app and a messaging app adds friction. A Cozi alternative that keeps the family calendar, task lists, and messaging in one place reduces the daily context-switching that fragments family coordination.

**An AI assistant that understands family life.** The newest generation of family apps includes an AI helper that can answer questions, help plan the week, and summarize what is outstanding. This is not a gimmick — for busy Swiss families managing dense schedules, a well-integrated assistant saves real time each week.
