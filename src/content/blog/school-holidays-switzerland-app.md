If you've recently moved to Switzerland, you've probably already had this experience: your child's school is on holiday, but the gym class you signed up for two cantons over is still running. Or you booked a trip assuming the autumn break matched what a friend in a different canton told you, only to find your school's dates were a week off.

Switzerland does not have a single national school holiday calendar. Each of the 26 cantons sets its own dates, often with only loose coordination between them. For Swiss families who grew up navigating this, it's a known quirk. For international and English-speaking families, it can feel like a moving target with no obvious source of truth.

## Why This Catches International Families Off Guard

Most school holiday information in Switzerland is published in German, French, or Italian, depending on the canton — rarely in English. A family relocating from the UK, the US, or elsewhere often discovers the holiday schedule the hard way: through a note sent home in a language they're still learning, or a deadline that's already passed.

On top of that, families with relatives or activities spanning multiple cantons — a child who attends an international school in one canton but has weekend sports in another — end up juggling two or three separate holiday calendars that were never designed to be compared side by side.

## The Problem With Tracking This Manually

The default approach is usually a mix of screenshots, browser bookmarks to cantonal education websites, and a mental note to "check again closer to the date." This works until it doesn't — usually right when a holiday week arrives and childcare hasn't been arranged, or a flight gets booked for dates that don't actually line up with the school calendar.

Google Calendar can hold the dates once you've found them, but it won't tell you what they are or remind you to organize holiday childcare in time. It's a place to store information, not a tool that helps you gather or act on it.

## What a Family App Should Actually Do Here

A family organizer app doesn't replace the cantonal authority that sets the dates, but it should make everything downstream of that date much easier to manage:

- **A shared calendar everyone in the family can see and edit**, so holiday dates, once entered, are visible to both parents, not just whoever found them first.
- **Task lists tied to holiday prep** — booking childcare, packing, confirming travel documents — so nothing slips through during a busy week.
- **Multilingual support**, so a notice from a French- or German-speaking school doesn't require a separate translation step before it goes into the calendar.
- **Privacy by default**, since travel dates and absence periods are exactly the kind of household information you don't want sitting inside an ad-supported platform.
