There's a lot of hype around AI assistants. But for families, the question isn't "is this AI smart?" — it's "does this AI understand what family life actually looks like?"

## What a Family AI Assistant Should Handle

### Shopping lists from natural language
Say "add oat milk, eggs, and the kids' shampoo to the shopping list" and it just works. No fumbling with an app interface while you're in the middle of cooking.

### Calendar events from conversation
"Add piano lesson every Tuesday at 4pm starting next week" should create the recurring event, not launch a form with 12 fields.

### Answering family questions
"What's on the schedule this weekend?" or "Did anyone add anything to the shopping list today?" — quick, conversational queries that don't require navigating to a specific screen.

### Multilingual support
In Swiss families especially, the AI needs to work in German, French, Italian, and English — often switching mid-conversation.

## What a Family AI Assistant Cannot (and Should Not) Do

- **Make decisions for you** — it can remind you about the dentist appointment, not decide whether to reschedule it
- **Replace human connection** — AI handles logistics so you have more time for the actual family moments
- **Know everything** — it only knows what you and your family have shared with it
